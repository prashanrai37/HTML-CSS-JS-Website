Mathematical Models

A mathematical model is a simplified representation of a real world situation. They are used to make predictions and by analysing them it provides users with a better understanding of the real situation. An example of mathematical model being used in computer science is scheduling done by operating systems. Mathematical models are useful because: They are quick and easy to produce They are easy to understand Variables can be readily changed making them versatile They are used to make predictions They can help provide control However they must be treated with caution as they tend to work in certain scenarios only and simplification of problems often leads to omission of variables
